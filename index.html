Our Philosophy

Fire, Tradition & Anatolia

'The Lokum' didn’t start as a restaurant idea. It started as a feeling; a taste captured in a distant memory and sought after for years.

We wanted the food that comes out of true Anatolian kitchens… passed down quietly through generations, guarded because the recipe means something deeply personal to the family. When our chef arrived from Turkey, he brought with him the rituals, the wood-fire techniques, and the very soul of Anatolia to Bengaluru.

Every dish starts with a moment of quiet intention. We cook close to the flame, letting the fire leave its mark. It's food that carries a whole country in its heart—designed so you can slow down, breathe, and experience Turkish hospitality in its truest form.

The Majlis

The Majlis at The Lokum is a sanctuary of togetherness — where conversations flow, time slows, and tradition comes alive. Inspired by the age-old Anatolian gathering, it is a curated space for shared meals, quiet reflections, and unforgettable evenings under a canopy of warm, amber light.
